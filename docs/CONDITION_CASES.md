# Case 1 — PCOS · Maya Chen, 34

## Slide one-liner
**Maya googles berberine before her GP explains her labs.**

## The human story (Patient Front Door)
- LH:FSH flagged **“borderline”** for years; testosterone **high**; periods irregular.
- Reads Reddit/Instagram: *berberine, spearmint, inositol* — doesn’t know what’s safe with her insulin picture.
- **Belief formed online:** “I should start berberine to fix blood sugar.”
- By appointment time, she’s already bought supplements — clinician has no structured context.

## Problem
| Patient | System |
|---------|--------|
| Acne, weight, fatigue, cycle chaos — feels dismissed | 8–10 yr average diagnosis delay |
| Confused by isolated lab flags | Hormones + metabolism rarely read together |
| High-risk self-treatment from search | No front door to catch supplement intent |

## Hormonal & metabolic biomarkers Hsence tracks
- **LH:FSH ratio** (e.g. 3.6 — PCOS-associated pattern)
- **Total / free testosterone**, **SHBG**
- **Progesterone** (anovulation signal)
- **Fasting glucose, insulin, HOMA-IR**
- **Lipids** (cardiometabolic risk)
- **Vitamin D, ferritin**
- **Wearable:** sleep, HRV, skin temp
- **Daily:** mood, food, supplement adherence

## What Hsence does (proactive)
1. **Intent:** `supplement_self_treatment` — berberine query → **standard care first**, weak evidence flagged.
2. **Pattern:** PCOS · elevated androgen · insulin resistance · suboptimal vitamin D.
3. **Four layers:** Hormones ↓ · Metabolic ↓ · Cognition (sleep) ↓ · Inflammation monitored.
4. **Care gaps (ranked):** Insulin resistance · androgen excess · vitamin D · cardiovascular lipids.
5. **Pathway:**
   - **Food:** low-GI, protein-forward meals (e.g. salmon + vegetables)
   - **Supplements ★:** myo-inositol 40:1 (strong PCOS evidence)
   - **Supplements ◈:** NAC, omega-3, magnesium, D3+K2 — graded
   - **Meds:** metformin discussion → **doctor referral only**
6. **Trials (demo):** myo-inositol vs metformin cohort — criterion match shown.
7. **Handoff:** *“Given my LH:FSH and testosterone, should we formally evaluate PCOS?”*

## Front-door double-check example
**Ask:** *“Should I take berberine for PCOS?”*  
**Hsence:** Does not endorse from search · cites weak/limited evidence · routes to HOMA-IR + metformin/inositol conversation · **Show why** + trace.

## Outcomes
| Patient | Clinical / Pfizer-aligned |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Understands labs in plain language | Faster path to **appropriate Rx** (metformin discussion) |
| Evidence-based supplement list | Better **adherence** with daily ritual |
| Arrives with structured questions | Higher-quality visit · **PA-ready** rationale (roadmap) |

## Slide bullets (PCOS)
- Borderline labs → **full hormonal pattern**
- Catches **berberine intent** before harm
- ★ inositol · gap-ranked insulin/androgen plan
- Trial + **GP handoff** in one cycle

**Demo persona:** Default `agent.html` — Maya Chen panel.

# Case 2 — Perimenopause · Helen Okonkwo, 48

## Slide one-liner
**Helen thinks brain fog is “just aging” — her hormones tell a different story.**

## The human story (Patient Front Door)
- Searches: *“perimenopause brain fog supplements”* · *“HRT yes or no”* · TikTok progesterone creams.
- **0 FDA-approved** meds for perimenopause cognitive symptoms — fills gap with unregulated products.
- Afraid to bother GP; minimizes symptoms.

## Problem
| Patient | System |
|---------|--------|
| Sleep fragmentation, fog, mood swings, weight | Symptoms attributed to stress or aging |
| Conflicting HRT advice online | Estrogen/progesterone changes rarely linked to sleep/HRV |
| Starts random “hormone balance” bundles | No longitudinal biomarker story |

## Hormonal & metabolic biomarkers Hsence tracks
- **Estradiol trend** (declining), **FSH** (rising perimenopause pattern)
- **Progesterone** (short luteal / anovulatory cycles)
- **Thyroid** (TSH — rule out look-alike fog)
- **Vitamin D, B12, ferritin** (fatigue mimics)
- **Glucose / lipids** (metabolic shift at menopause)
- **Wearable:** sleep architecture, HRV, **temperature deviation**
- **Daily:** cognition check-in, mood, morning brew narrative

## What Hsence does (proactive)
1. **Intent:** symptom_seeking + supplement_self_treatment risk.
2. **Pattern:** perimenopause · estrogen transition · sleep-driven cognition load.
3. **Four layers:** Cognition ↓ · Hormones ↓ · Metabolic (watch) · Inflammation (recovery).
4. **Care gaps:** sleep disorder screen · thyroid check · bone density (DEXA) · HRT eligibility discussion.
5. **Pathway:**
   - **Food:** protein breakfast, stable glucose, anti-inflammatory plates
   - **Supplements ◈:** magnesium, omega-3, vitamin D3+K2 (bone + mood support)
   - **Meds:** **HRT / non-hormonal Rx** — clinician-only; Hsence prepares questions
6. **Morning brew:** *“Fragmented sleep + temp shift — protect focus before noon.”*
7. **Handoff:** *“Is my fog hormonal, thyroid, or sleep? Should we discuss HRT risks/benefits for me?”*

## Front-door double-check example
**Ask:** *“Which progesterone cream should I buy online?”*  
**Hsence:** Flags unregulated self-treatment · routes to **prescription HRT conversation** · no product endorsement.

## Outcomes
| Patient | Clinical |
|---------|----------|
| Names experience (not “just aging”) | Earlier **appropriate therapy** discussion |
| Sleep + HRV context for visits | Continuous monitoring replaces yearly snapshot |
| Bone/metabolic risks surfaced early | **DEXA / cardiovascular** gaps closed |

## Slide bullets (Perimenopause)
- Fog + sleep + **wearable fusion**
- Blocks unsafe **online HRT shortcuts**
- Cognition layer + **bone/metabolic** gaps
- **Morning brew** — one daily read

**Demo:** Home → morning brew → **perimenopause · brain fog** mode.

# Case 3 — Gestational diabetes · Sofia Reyes, 28

## Slide one-liner
**Sofia’s glucose spikes between midwife visits — no one explained what to eat.**

## The human story (Patient Front Door)
- 26 weeks pregnant; fasting glucose and post-meal spikes.
- Googles: *“GD meal plan”* · *“can I eat fruit?”* — conflicting answers.
- **Fear + guilt** about harming baby; inconsistent adherence.

## Problem
| Patient | System |
|---------|--------|
| Spikes between appointments | Prenatal visits are weeks apart |
| Generic diet sheets | No fusion with **activity, sleep, stress** |
| Tempted by “natural blood sugar” supplements | **Pregnancy safety** rarely enforced in apps |

## Hormonal & metabolic biomarkers Hsence tracks
- **Fasting glucose**, **HbA1c**, post-meal logs (daily)
- **Pregnancy-specific** insulin sensitivity (rising insulin resistance)
- **Wearable:** steps, sleep, stress proxy (HRV)
- **Daily:** meal timing, food categories, mood
- **No unsafe supplements** — pregnancy guardrail

## What Hsence does (proactive)
1. **Intent:** symptom_seeking + food guidance (low risk).
2. **Pattern:** gestational diabetes · post-meal glucose variability.
3. **Four layers:** Metabolic ↓ · Inflammation (watch) · Cognition (stress/sleep) · Hormones (pregnancy context).
4. **Care gaps:** nutrition referral · glucose log review · postpartum diabetes screen plan.
5. **Pathway:**
   - **Food:** low-GI, timed meals, protein + fiber pairing
   - **Meal timing plan** between visits
   - **Supplements:** only pregnancy-safe, clinician-approved (Hsence does **not** auto-recommend herbals)
   - **Meds:** insulin/metformin in pregnancy → **midwife/obstetrician only**
6. **Handoff:** *“What glucose targets should I use? When do we escalate to medication?”*

## Front-door double-check example
**Ask:** *“Can I take berberine while pregnant for blood sugar?”*  
**Hsence:** **Hard safety route** — not recommended in pregnancy · escalate to clinician immediately.

## Outcomes
| Patient | Clinical |
|---------|----------|
| Actionable meal plan daily | Better **adherence**, fewer spikes |
| Questions ready for midwife | Faster **treatment escalation** when needed |
| Postpartum screen remembered | Prevents **type 2 diabetes** after birth |

## Slide bullets (GDM)
- **Between-visit** glucose support
- Pregnancy-safe · **no risky supplements**
- Meal timing + **low-GI rules**
- **Midwife handoff** questions

**Demo:** `daily.html` food step + quote on home page (Sofia).

# Case 4 — Osteoporosis · Helen Park, 58 (post-menopause)

## Slide one-liner
**Helen’s vitamin D was “fine” — but no one scheduled her DEXA.**

## The human story (Patient Front Door)
- Post-menopause; family history of hip fracture.
- Searches: *“best calcium supplement”* · *“vitamin D megadose”* — overdose risk.
- Avoids HRT; unsure about bone meds (bisphosphonates).

## Problem
| Patient | System |
|---------|--------|
| Estrogen decline → bone loss | **DEXA follow-up** often missed |
| Takes random calcium without K2/Mg context | Labs (D, PTH, calcium) not tied to **fracture risk** |
| Fear of bone medications | No shared decision support |

## Hormonal & metabolic biomarkers Hsence tracks
- **Vitamin D**, **calcium**, **PTH**, **phosphate**
- **Estradiol** (post-menopausal range)
- **Inflammation** markers (recovery, comorbidity)
- **Wearable:** weight-bearing activity, falls risk proxy (activity drop)
- **Daily:** adherence to D3+K2, protein intake

## What Hsence does (proactive)
1. **Pattern:** post-menopause · suboptimal vitamin D · bone density risk.
2. **Four layers:** Hormones (estrogen decline) ↓ · Metabolic · Cognition · Inflammation.
3. **Care gaps:** **DEXA overdue** · vitamin D repletion · weight-bearing exercise · medication review.
4. **Pathway:**
   - **Supplements ★/◈:** D3+K2, calcium **food-first**, magnesium
   - **Lifestyle:** resistance + weight-bearing cues from wearable
   - **Meds:** bisphosphonates / denosumab → **endocrinology or GP referral**
5. **Handoff:** *“When is my next DEXA? Do I need bone medication given my fracture risk?”*

## Front-door double-check example
**Ask:** *“Should I take 10,000 IU vitamin D daily?”*  
**Hsence:** Checks current level · warns on megadose self-treatment · **retest plan** + clinician review.

## Outcomes
| Patient | Clinical |
|---------|----------|
| Imaging gap surfaced | Earlier **fracture prevention** |
| Safe D3+K2 plan | Avoids **hypercalcemia** from blind dosing |
| Activity nudges | Adherence to **weight-bearing** exercise |

## Slide bullets (Osteoporosis)
- Estrogen decline + **D/PTH** fusion
- **DEXA gap** alerts
- D3+K2 · food-first calcium
- **Bone med** conversation via doctor

# Case 5 — Hormonal cancer · survivorship · Rachel Kim, 41

## Slide one-liner
**Rachel finished treatment — surveillance and trials feel like a second full-time job.**

## The human story (Patient Front Door)
- Breast cancer survivorship; hormone-sensitive tumor history.
- Searches: *“tamoxifen side effects”* · *“clinical trials near me”* — overwhelming criteria.
- Inflammation, fatigue, fear of recurrence; **60% of trial effort** is eligibility screening.

## Problem
| Patient | System |
|---------|--------|
| Surveillance appointments easy to miss | **Care gaps** in imaging/labs |
| Trial websites incomprehensible | Criteria up **58%** in two decades |
| Supplement culture for “immune boost” | Conflicts with oncology plan |

## Hormonal & metabolic biomarkers Hsence tracks
- **Treatment timeline** (surgery, chemo, endocrine therapy)
- **Estradiol / endocrine therapy** context (not fertility-focused)
- **Inflammation layer** (recovery, CRP trend if available)
- **Cognition + sleep** (chemo brain, fatigue)
- **Daily:** energy, mood, adherence to endocrine therapy

## What Hsence does (proactive)
1. **Intent:** trial_interest · clinician_prep · care_gap.
2. **Pattern:** survivorship · inflammation recovery · hormone-sensitive follow-up.
3. **Four layers:** Inflammation · Cognition · Hormones (therapy side effects) · Metabolic.
4. **Care gaps:** surveillance imaging · endocrine adherence · cardio-metabolic monitoring.
5. **Pathway:**
   - **No unsupervised “immune” supplements** — oncology-safe only
   - **Trial matching (demo):** criterion-by-criterion ✓/✗ with rationale
   - **Handoff:** evidence-backed **oncology questions**
6. **Guardrail:** explicitly **not diagnosis** · not staging · surveillance support only.

## Front-door double-check example
**Ask:** *“Should I stop tamoxifen and take natural estrogen balance supplements?”*  
**Hsence:** **High-risk** · does not support altering oncology Rx · urgent **oncologist handoff**.

## Outcomes
| Patient | Clinical / research |
|---------|---------------------|
| Trial options explained | Faster **enrollment** screening (TrialMatchAI analogue) |
| Surveillance gaps visible | Fewer **missed imaging** events |
| Traceable eligibility | **Explainable** trial rationale for coordinators |

## Slide bullets (Survivorship)
- Surveillance + **inflammation** layers
- **Trial criteria** row-by-row
- Blocks dangerous **Rx changes** from search
- **Oncology handoff** — CDSS not diagnosis
